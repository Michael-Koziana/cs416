Impact of Covid on Economic Growth

Is the spread of Covid a good predictor of Economic Growth in the year 2021 and 2022?


Fundamental Questions:


Is the death rate per 100,000 a good indictator of economic growth or decline?

Is the vaccination rate of the country a good metric of growth or decline?


Project Objective and Background:


As we all know, Covid wreaked havoc on the world in the year 2020 and till this very day, and it left numerous consequences with it, including many socioeconomic problems.

This project explores the idea that there might be casual links between covid cases and/or vaccinations to overall economic growth trends and forecasts for the years 2021 and 2022.

Does the spread of covid inhibit economies? Is the vaccine a reliable indictator of the forecast economic growth of 2020 and 2021? The goal of this project is to explore these questions and visualize some components! While this is a loaded question, there might be some correlation between various datapoints!


Data Sources:


The first dataset used in the first chart on the second page comes from the World Bank. It includes numerous attributes relating to GDP. This World Bank dataset is publicly available at the given hyperlink.

The second dataset used is from the International Monetary Fund. It includes numerous attributes relating to GDP. This IMF dataset is publicly available at the hyperlink.

The third dataset used is from Our World in Data Covid Metrics. It includes numerous attributes including: vaccination numbers, population, total covid cases, new covid cases. This dataset used is a subsection of covid datapoints on 12/31/2020 from Our World In Data and is publicly available.

The fourth dataset used is a merge of the second and third dataset. It includes numerous attributes including: vaccination numbers, population, total covid cases, new covid cases alongside economic growth rates. This dataset used is a subsection of covid datapoints on 7/31/2021 from Our World In Data and the IMF.
